Question 3 Let say we take sample of size 30 from the geometric distribution with some chosen parameter and repeat that 200 times. Draw a plot of the distribution of Xn,that is draw a histogram of the 200 values of sample means. What are expected value and variance of Xn? What distribution do you get - name it. Hint: some important theorem tells you what the distribution is.in R geometric distribution is denoted as P(X = k) = p (1 - p)^k ,which means that the expected value is equal to E(X) = 1p/p.

p <- 0.3
n <- 30
B <- 200

set.seed(123)
sample_means <- replicate(B, mean(rgeom(n, prob = p)))

hist(sample_means,
     breaks = 20,
     col = "lightblue",
     main = "Distribution of Sample Means (Geometric)",
     xlab = "Sample Mean")

expected_value <- (1 - p) / p
variance_single <- (1 - p) / (p^2)
variance_mean <- variance_single / n

cat("Expected value of Xbar_n:", round(expected_value, 2), "\n")
## Expected value of Xbar_n: 2.33
cat("Variance of Xbar_n:", round(variance_mean, 2), "\n")
## Variance of Xbar_n: 0.26

The histogram shows that the distribution of these means is approximately normal in shape. This aligns with the Central Limit Theorem, which tells us that the distribution of sample means tends to be normal regardless of the populations shape, as long as the sample size is reasonably large. The sample mean (~2.33) matches the theoretical value, and the variance (~0.26) shows reduced spread due to averaging. Despite the geometric distribution being skewed, the sample means follow a normal pattern when n=30.

  1. Generate two samples of size 50 from a normal distribution that will represent your data before and after treatment. Use an appropriate statistical test for = 0.05 and perform a statistical analysis. Does the treatment work?
set.seed(123)

before <- rnorm(50, mean = 8, sd = 1)
after  <- rnorm(50, mean = 7.5, sd = 1)

# Paired t-test
t_test_result <- t.test(before, after, paired = TRUE)

print(t_test_result)
## 
##  Paired t-test
## 
## data:  before and after
## t = 2.0815, df = 49, p-value = 0.04263
## alternative hypothesis: true mean difference is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  0.01341443 0.76257613
## sample estimates:
## mean difference 
##       0.3879953
if (t_test_result$p.value < 0.05) {
  cat("Conclusion: There is a statistically significant reduction in blood sugar levels (p < 0.05).\n")
  cat("The treatment appears effective.\n")
} else {
  cat("Conclusion: No statistically significant difference (p  0.05).\n")
  cat("There is no evidence the treatment made a difference.\n")
}
## Conclusion: There is a statistically significant reduction in blood sugar levels (p < 0.05).
## The treatment appears effective.

The paired t-test resulted in a p-value of 0.04263, which is less than the significance level 0.05. This means we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that there is a statistically significant difference in blood sugar levels before and after treatment.Since the mean difference is positive (0.39) and we measured before after, this suggests that blood sugar levels decreased after treatment.
